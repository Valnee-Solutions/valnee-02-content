## The Firm

Valnee Solutions is a software agency that works with founders at two specific stages: before
a product exists, and after a product exists but is causing problems. We do not take every
project that comes in. We work with a small number of clients each quarter and assign senior
practitioners to every engagement. The work we deliver is documented, owned by the client,
and built to be handed off cleanly.

The firm is founder-led. There is no bench of junior developers receiving task assignments
from a project manager who never writes code. The people doing the work are the people you
talk to during scoping, during delivery, and during the post-delivery review.

## The Founder

Arshad has been building software products for a decade across logistics, fintech, and
B2B SaaS. His early career was in backend engineering on high-volume transaction systems —
the kind of work where an error rate of 0.1 percent is not acceptable because it means
thousands of real failures per day. That background shaped how he approaches product
architecture: with a bias toward systems that fail predictably, fail visibly, and fail in
ways that can be fixed without a production emergency.

Over time, Arshad moved from engineering into technical leadership — leading teams, making
architecture decisions, and acting as a fractional technical co-founder for several
early-stage companies. In that role, he sat across the table from founders who had been
badly served by agencies and freelancers: projects delivered late, codebases no one else
could maintain, scope that expanded without warning, and documentation that did not exist.
The pattern was consistent enough that it felt less like a series of bad actors and more
like a structural problem with how most agencies are run.

Valnee was built to operate differently. Smaller client load. Senior practitioners on every
engagement. Scope defined in writing before work begins. Documentation produced continuously
rather than compressed into the final days of a project. A post-delivery review built into
every engagement by default. None of these are novel ideas — they are just practices that
most firms say they follow but few actually enforce. Making them non-negotiable is what the
firm is organized around.

Today, Valnee works on a small number of active engagements at any given time. The pipeline
is short by design. Founders who join the waitlist typically move to a scoping call within
two to four weeks of submitting their intake form. The firm has no plans to scale beyond the
point where every active engagement can receive direct senior attention.

## What Valnee Is Not

Understanding what a firm will not do is as informative as understanding what it will.

- **Not a dev shop with a bench.** Valnee does not maintain a pool of available junior developers
  who are assigned to projects as they come in. Every engagement is staffed with senior
  practitioners who have been assessed on technical depth and communication quality.

- **Not an offshore delivery center.** Valnee does not compete on rate arbitrage. The value
  is in the quality of the work, the documentation, and the accountability — not in the
  per-hour cost of the labor.

- **Not a design agency that builds on the side.** Software engineering is the primary
  discipline here. Design, copywriting, and brand work are outside the scope of what Valnee
  does, and Valnee will not pretend otherwise to close a deal.

- **Not a product studio taking equity.** Valnee is paid for its work at market rates. The
  firm does not take equity positions in client companies. This keeps the relationship clean:
  Valnee's incentive is to deliver excellent work, not to optimize for a particular outcome
  in your cap table.

- **Not a full-service managed services provider.** Valnee builds and hands off. Ongoing
  maintenance, monitoring, and support after delivery are available in limited form through
  the developer-on-demand model, but the default at the end of every engagement is that the
  client owns and operates what was built.

## Working Principles

These are the operational commitments that govern how every Valnee engagement runs. They
are not aspirational values — they are the actual standards work is held to.

### Scope in writing, always.

Before any code is written, the scope of the engagement is documented and agreed on in
writing. Changes to scope after that point are handled through a written change request that
includes the time and cost implications. This protects both parties: the client knows exactly
what they are getting, and Valnee is not in the position of absorbing work that was never
agreed on. Most conflicts between agencies and clients trace back to ambiguous scope. The
written scope document eliminates the ambiguity before it becomes a conflict.

### You own the code from day one.

The repository lives in the client's version control account from the first commit. Valnee
does not maintain custody of the codebase on behalf of the client and transfer it at the
end. If the engagement ends for any reason — completion, early termination, or otherwise —
the client has full access to everything that has been built up to that point. There is no
proprietary tooling, no Valnee-specific dependency, and no licensing arrangement that
restricts how the client uses the delivered system.

### Documentation is a deliverable, not a footnote.

Every engagement ends with a handoff package: internal architecture documentation, API
documentation where applicable, a deployment runbook, and a recorded walkthrough of the
delivered system. This documentation is written continuously throughout the engagement, not
compressed into the final week. The standard used is that a competent developer who has
never seen the codebase should be able to understand the system, deploy it, and extend it
using only the documentation provided. If that standard is not met at delivery, the
documentation is the deficiency to fix, not the developer's problem to solve on their own.

### Decisions are explained, not just made.

Every significant technical decision — stack selection, architecture choices, data model
design, third-party service selection — is recorded with the alternatives considered and
the reasons for the choice. For non-technical founders, these records are written in
plain language. The goal is not to require the client to understand every technical detail.
The goal is to ensure that if the client ever wants to understand a decision, the record
exists. This also means that future developers inheriting the codebase do not have to
reverse-engineer the reasoning behind how things were built.

### No surprises on timeline or cost.

If something happens during an engagement that will affect the timeline or the cost — a
technical discovery that changes the scope, a dependency that behaves differently than
expected, a client feedback cycle that requires more revision than anticipated — Valnee
communicates it immediately in writing. The client receives a revised projection and an
explanation before the change affects the project, not after. Most agencies handle surprises
retrospectively. Valnee handles them prospectively.
