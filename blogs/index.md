This blog covers the practical side of building software products as a founder — what
actually goes wrong, what decisions are harder than they look, and what patterns consistently
work. The content is written by Valnee's practitioners and draws on real engagement
experience. We do not publish advice that we have not tested, and we do not cover topics
for SEO purposes. If a post is on this blog, it is because we have seen the problem it
describes enough times to have a view worth sharing.

Topics covered include MVP strategy, technical architecture decisions for early-stage
companies, codebase maintenance, developer hiring, and the mechanics of building a
software company without a technical co-founder. Posts are tagged by the audience they
are most relevant to.
