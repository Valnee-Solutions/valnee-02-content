## Senior developers, available now.

The developer store is for founders and engineering leads who need vetted senior development
capacity without the overhead of a full hiring process. Engagements are hourly or weekly,
with a defined minimum duration and clear terms. You review the developer's background before
committing, and you can extend or end the engagement on terms agreed at the start.

This is not a staffing agency model where a coordinator places whichever body is available.
Every developer in the store has been assessed by Valnee on technical depth, communication
quality, and the ability to integrate into an existing codebase without extensive context-setting.
You are not hiring the cheapest available option — you are hiring a senior practitioner who
will be accountable for specific deliverables from week one.

## Engagement Terms

```
INTERNAL NOTE: Engagement terms section. Clear, structured, no ambiguity. The client is
likely to have been burned by unclear contractor arrangements before. Be specific about
minimums, payment, communication, and what happens if it is not working.
```

**Minimum engagement duration:** Two weeks (80 hours at hourly rate, or two weekly blocks).
This minimum exists because meaningful integration into an existing codebase requires a
ramp-up period. One-week engagements rarely produce enough value to justify the coordination
overhead for either party.

**Hourly versus weekly:** Hourly billing is available for engagements that are genuinely
variable in scope — for example, code review sessions, specific problem-solving calls, or
advisory work. For development work with defined deliverables, weekly billing is standard.
Weekly billing provides the developer with the predictability to plan focused work, and
provides the client with a predictable cost.

**Payment terms:** Payment is due at the start of each week for weekly engagements, or
at the start of each billing period for hourly engagements. Invoices are issued the business
day before the billing period begins. Payment by bank transfer or card.

**Communication:** Developers in the Valnee store operate on the same communication protocol
as standard Valnee engagements: async-first, written updates at the end of each working
week, and a documented end-of-week summary describing what was completed, what is in progress,
and what the client needs to review or decide. Daily check-in calls are available if the
client requires them, but the default is weekly structured written updates.

**Extending an engagement:** An engagement can be extended by notifying the developer and
Valnee at least three business days before the current engagement end date. Extensions are
confirmed in writing with the revised end date and the same rate as the original engagement
unless circumstances have changed.

**Ending an engagement early:** An engagement can be ended early with three business days
written notice. Payment is due for all work completed up to and including the notice period.
If the engagement is ended because the developer's work does not meet the agreed standard,
that dispute is handled through a defined review process — see below.

**If something is not working:** If the quality of work or the communication is not meeting
expectations, the client raises the concern in writing to Valnee directly. Valnee reviews
the concern, discusses it with the developer, and responds within two business days with
an assessment and a proposed resolution. If the resolution is not satisfactory, the
engagement can be terminated on one week's notice with a proportional refund for work not
yet delivered.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly can a developer start?

Most developers in the store can start within three to seven business days of a confirmed
booking. Availability is shown on each profile and is updated weekly. If you have a specific
start date in mind, mention it when you book — if the developer is not available by that
date, we will tell you immediately rather than accepting the booking and disappointing you
closer to the date.

### Can I extend an engagement?

Yes, with three business days' notice before the current engagement end date. Extensions are
confirmed in writing at the same rate unless both parties agree to a revised arrangement.
Most developers prefer to know as early as possible that an engagement is being extended,
as it affects their availability planning. If you think you will want to extend, let us know
early in the engagement.

### Do I get to evaluate the developer before committing?

Yes. Before confirming a booking, you can request a 30-minute technical discussion with the
developer to assess fit. This call is free. The purpose is not a technical interview in the
traditional sense — the developer's background has already been assessed by Valnee. The
purpose is to establish communication style, understand the specific work you need done, and
confirm that both sides are confident in the match before payment is committed.

### What stacks are available?

The current developer profiles cover Node.js, Python, React, Next.js, TypeScript, React
Native, iOS, PostgreSQL, Redis, AWS, GCP, Docker, Kubernetes, and CI/CD pipeline tooling.
Stack coverage changes as profiles are updated. If you need a specific technology that is not
represented in the current profiles, contact Valnee directly — the available roster is not
limited to what is published on this page at any given time.

### Is developer-on-demand appropriate for a long-term arrangement?

Engagements of three months or longer are available and common. Beyond six months, it is
worth discussing whether a transition to a full-time hire or a standard project engagement
might better serve your needs — not because Valnee wants to end the arrangement, but because
the economics and the relationship dynamics of a very long-term contractor arrangement can
start to create ambiguities that are better resolved through a different structure. Valnee
will raise this proactively if it seems relevant, and will help with the transition if you
decide to hire the developer directly.
