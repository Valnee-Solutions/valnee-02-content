## Hourly developers. No hiring process. Full accountability.

The Developer Store is for founders who need execution now — specific work, defined scope, and no time for a full project engagement.

You describe the project. We assign the right developer from our team. Work is billed hourly, with a transparent log of hours submitted by the developer and reviewed by you before each payment.

There is no catalogue to browse and no developer to select. We do the matching. Our team is assessed on technical depth, communication clarity, and the ability to integrate into an existing codebase without extensive hand-holding.

**Step 1: Submit your project brief**
Tell us what you need built or fixed. Include the stack, the scope, and your target timeline. Use the [project brief form](waitlist.md) or email us directly at hello@valnee.com.

**Step 2: We review and assign**
We review your brief and assign the developer from our team whose skills and availability are the best match for your project. You'll receive a short profile and confirmation before work begins.

**Step 3: Work begins**
The developer starts on the agreed date. All work is billed hourly. The developer tracks and submits their hours on a regular basis — you have full visibility into the time logged before any payment is processed.

**Step 4: You pay Valnee**
Billing runs through Valnee. You pay us based on verified hours at the agreed rate. We handle payment to the developer on our side. There are no hidden fees.

## What to Expect

**Hourly billing, not milestones.**
Unlike a fixed-price project engagement, the Developer Store runs on hourly billing. Rates are confirmed per project before work begins — they depend on the technical scope, stack, and required experience level. No rate is applied without your agreement.

**Transparent hours.**
The developer logs hours as work proceeds. You receive a summary of hours completed before each billing cycle. You're not paying a lump sum in the dark — you see exactly what was worked on and when.

**Async-first communication.**
Developers provide a written end-of-week summary: what was completed, what's in progress, and anything that needs your review or decision. Daily check-in calls are available if needed, but written updates are the default.

**Defined engagement terms.**
Every engagement has a written agreement covering the rate, the start date, the notice period for ending the engagement, and the process for resolving disputes. Nothing is left informal.

---

## Engagement Terms

**Minimum duration:** Two weeks. Meaningful work on an existing codebase requires ramp-up time. One-week engagements rarely produce enough value for either side.

**Extending an engagement:** Notify us at least three business days before the current end date. Extensions are confirmed in writing at the same rate.

**Ending an engagement early:** Three business days written notice. You pay for all hours worked up to and including the notice period.

**If something isn't working:** Raise it in writing directly to Valnee. We review the concern, discuss it with the developer, and respond within two business days with an assessment and proposed resolution.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How quickly can work start?**
Most engagements can begin within three to seven business days of a confirmed brief. If you have a specific start date, mention it — we'll confirm availability before accepting the project.

**What if the developer isn't the right fit?**
We take matching seriously. If there's a genuine mismatch in the first week, raise it directly with us and we'll assess whether a reassignment is appropriate. We don't expect this to be a common situation, but the process exists.

**What kinds of work does the Developer Store handle?**
Backend and frontend development, API integrations, DevOps and deployment, mobile development, codebase cleanup, and short-term team augmentation. If you're unsure whether your project is a fit, describe it and we'll tell you directly.

**Is this appropriate for longer-term work?**
Yes. Engagements of several months are available and common. For very long-term arrangements, it's worth discussing whether a different structure better serves your needs — we'll raise that proactively if it becomes relevant.
